One of the most common questions we receive is simple: *Should I choose a guided trip or a self-guided itinerary?*

The answer depends less on fitness, and more on the type of travel experience you want.

## Guided journeys

A guided format works best when you want interpretation, rhythm and local depth.

You walk with someone who can connect the route to geology, land use, migration patterns and village history in real time. For many travellers, that turns a beautiful hike into a memorable learning experience.

## Self-guided journeys

A self-guided format gives more autonomy while still benefiting from local route design.

You receive clear stage planning, logistics support and trail context, but move at your own pace. It is ideal for independent walkers who value flexibility without sacrificing quality of route design.

## A practical way to choose

Choose guided if you prioritise:

- interpretation and storytelling on the trail
- structured group rhythm
- lower planning responsibility

Choose self-guided if you prioritise:

- independence and private pace
- flexible timing each day
- still having strong local route design behind the scenes

Both formats share the same foundation: serious route curation in protected landscapes, with a focus on nature and cultural depth.
