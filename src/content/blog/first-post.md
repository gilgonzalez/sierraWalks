At SierraWalks we often say that these mountains are not just scenic: they are a meeting point of worlds.

Within the **Mediterranean Intercontinental Biosphere Reserve (UNESCO)**, geology, climate and migration routes combine to create one of the most singular territories in southern Europe. You feel this quickly on foot: one valley carries Atlantic humidity, while the next ridge turns dry and Mediterranean.

This contrast is not abstract science. It shapes what you see every day on the trail:

- cork oak forests and high limestone ridges within short distances
- bird migration corridors linked to the Straits of Gibraltar
- villages, terraces and ancient pathways adapted to mountain life

For us, walking holidays should explain a place, not only cross it.

That is why our routes are designed around context: local history, protected habitats, and the practical realities of rural Andalucia. A good itinerary is not measured only in kilometres, but in how clearly it helps travellers read a landscape.

When guests finish a journey, we want them to remember more than viewpoints. We want them to understand *why* these sierras look the way they do, and why protecting them matters.
