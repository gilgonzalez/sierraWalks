In south-western Andalucia, the quality of a walking holiday is closely tied to seasonality.

The same route can feel open, floral and expansive in spring, then quieter, drier and more architectural in autumn. Neither is inherently better. They simply reveal different aspects of the same landscape.

## Spring

Spring is often the season that first draws walkers to the sierras.

There is usually more visible water in the landscape, greener slopes, and a sense of movement across habitats as temperatures remain relatively comfortable for longer days on the trail.

## Autumn

Autumn brings a different clarity.

The terrain can feel more sculptural, village life often feels more pronounced after summer, and the lower angle of light gives the mountains a particularly calm, editorial beauty.

## How we think about timing

We design routes with seasonal reading in mind, not just accommodation logistics.

That means thinking about:

- daily exposure and shade
- likely trail conditions
- how certain landscapes are best experienced at different times of year
- the pace needed for travellers who want to look, not rush

A good walking holiday is rarely just about *where* you go. Very often, it is about *when* you go and how well the route respects that moment in the landscape.
