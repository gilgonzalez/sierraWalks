The landscapes around the Straits of Gibraltar are not only dramatic to walk through, they are also biologically strategic.

During migration periods, this region becomes a major movement corridor between continents. Even on a normal walking day, you may notice how alive the sky and coast can feel.

## Why this area is so important

This part of south-western Andalucia sits close to one of Europe’s most significant migration bottlenecks.

Combined with protected mountain systems inland, it creates an unusual concentration of biodiversity for a relatively compact territory.

## What this means for a walking itinerary

At SierraWalks, we do not build routes around speed. We build them around attention.

That means choosing trail sections with room to pause, observe and understand context:

- ridgelines with long visual range
- transitions between habitats
- historical pathways that connect villages and uplands

## Walking as a way of seeing

For us, migration is not just a specialist subject for birders. It is part of the wider story of place.

When you stand on a windy pass, watching movement through the sky while looking toward the coast and inland sierras, you begin to understand why this territory matters globally and locally at the same time.

That layered perspective is the core of how we design walking holidays.
